Tips

Plan First: Create an outline with your thesis, main points, and evidence.
Show, Don’t Tell: Use concrete details and sensory language.
Quote Integration: Introduce, quote, then explain relevance.
Vary Sentence Structure: Mix simple, compound, and complex sentences.
Proofread: Check grammar, punctuation, and MLA formatting.

Examples

Thesis Example

"In Macbeth, Shakespeare uses irony to reveal the corrosive effects of unchecked ambition on the human soul."

Topic Sentence Example

"The episodic structure of The Odyssey highlights Odysseus’s growth as both a leader and individual through a series of trials."

Poetry Excerpt

"Beneath the silver moon’s pale glow I wandered roads I used to know." (Imagery, end rhyme, enjambment)

Speech Outline

Ethos: Cite research credentials
Pathos: Share a personal story
Logos: Present statistics
Conclusion: Call to action
